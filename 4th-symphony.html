4th Symphony Orchestra Poster

Research-Based Print Design
Student Exercise
Inspiration: Josef Müller-Brockmann

This exercise is more of a visual study of Swiss International Design than a truly original work, but it represents what I consider to be a significant breakthrough for me in my design education. Although prior to this exercise, I was familiar with the concept of grid in design, the idea had not really “come to life.” Using grid structure made logical sense, but I was hardly excited by the concept. However, as I was studying the history of graphic design, I was introduced to the Swiss International Style for the first time, and with it, the more subtle and exciting uses of grid structure, especially paired with typography.

This poster was made around the time this idea started coming together for me, and I think it conveys all my excitement and exploration of grid, with each element altering and aligning to the page’s structure. Although typically a bit less wild than this poster, the grid has been prevalent in all of my works since.
