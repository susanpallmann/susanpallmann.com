Cardboard Ukulele

3D Design & Craft
Student Project

There was less given in the way of objective for this project. Simply, one of the prompt options was to create a 3D object that makes sound.

Being the ambitious person I am by nature, I couldn’t resist the opportunity to make what could be an entry level 3D task into a passion project, and explore another love of mine – music. Having been musically trained for 12 years of my life, I was enticed by the possibility of making a real instrument, a concert ukulele, to form and function. However, given the opportunity to also be experimental (and stuck on a college student budget), I elected to build the entire body out of cardboard.

The only non-cardboard elements are the bridge, frets, tuners, and of course, strings. Despite the unusual materials, the instrument plays and tunes just like a real ukulele would, a successful design and execution.
